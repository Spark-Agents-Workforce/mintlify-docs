## Pattern C — The play (Steps wrapper)

The script embedded in its run-order: Steps for the ritual around the send. Best where sequence IS the content (5.4 calendar, 1.3 send order).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pull your number first">
    Your leverage is [$Y]/year. If you skipped it, [Know your number](/instant-win/know-your-number) takes four minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send Script 01">
    The letterhead block from Pattern A sits here, copy button and all.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Calendar the silence">
    No reply in 5 business days → Script 02. Their silence is a move; you have a counter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pattern D — Objection table (for "What they'll say back")

Accordion per reply-type: scannable index of their moves, expandable counters. The companion pattern — 1.4 is a decision tree, not a sequence.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='"That rate is standard for accounts your size."'>
    **What it means:** opening anchor, not a position. **Counter — Script 03A:**
    ```text
    Appreciate the context. "Standard" is what we're re-evaluating —
    [COMPETITOR] quotes flat licensing at our volume...
    ```
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title='"We can offer 10% off if you sign for two years."'>
    **What it means:** they're trading price for lock-in — churn insurance for them, not savings for you. **Counter — Script 03B.**
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Silence.">
    **What it means:** deprioritized, betting you won't follow through. **Counter — Script 02, CC their manager.**
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The recommendation

A is the spine (every script page), B layers on when copy forks per incumbent, C wraps where order matters, D owns 1.4. One system, four moves.
