## The folklore this lesson kills

Most dashboards bucket "paused" next to "cancelled" and call the sum churn. That single grouping decision quietly costs brands their easiest revenue. **A paused subscriber didn't reject you — they asked for room.** They have product left. They're traveling. Their cadence was slightly wrong. Filed under "churned," they get either ignored or hit with the same apologetic winback discount you'd send a hard cancel — insulting to someone who never left, and a discount you didn't need to give.

Paused is not churned. Paused is **the warmest list you own** — warmer than any lookalike audience you will ever buy, because these people are one nudge from a resume, not one pitch from a first purchase. This lesson is about working that list gently, and about a harder truth the store's numbers force into view: even a warm resurrection list is dwarfed by prevention.

## The decision: nudge the resume, don't sell the return

The paused segment runs on a different instrument than cancels. Three moves, in order of how much you're allowed to ask:

- **The auto-resume nudge.** Before a pause auto-expires, a low-pressure heads-up: *your next order is scheduled for [date] — need to change anything?* No offer. The job is to convert a scheduled resume into a *welcomed* resume so it doesn't become a cancel the day the charge lands. (The store's data shows resume *events* but no resume-nudge cadence or response data — the timing here is a starting point to test, not a measured cadence.)
- **Pause-extension over reactivation offer — the counterintuitive move.** When someone still has product, the right offer is often *more room, not a discount.* Extending the pause (or letting them skip again) keeps the subscription alive at zero margin cost and avoids the cancellation that a badly-timed charge would have triggered. You reach for a reactivation *incentive* only when the relationship has actually gone cold — not when it's merely paused. Giving a discount to someone who only needed another three weeks is pure margin donation.
- **The reactivation offer, reserved.** Same discipline as [Recapturing cancelled](/growth-program/winback/recapturing-cancelled): the incentive is the last lever, for genuinely lapsed pauses that ignored the nudges.

## The proof (one measured case, hedges attached)

Two things are true in this store's data at once, and the tension between them *is* the lesson.

**First: the reactivation motion is real and accelerating.** Over roughly six months the store logged about **1,200 reactivations** and **700 resumes** — meaningful volume, not noise. The save/resume signal has been climbing since instrumentation began: the save/resume ratio roughly **quintupled** (about 1.3% to 5.7%) across three quarters. A resume/reactivation habit is forming, and it's worth building flows around.

**Second — and this is the number that reframes the whole module: cancellations dwarf both.** Over that same half-year the store logged roughly **37,000 cancellations** against those ~1,200 reactivations and ~700 resumes combined. Resurrection recovers on the order of **one subscriber for every twenty that leave.** (Hedge: these are lifecycle *event* counts, not distinct-subscriber churn — creates and cancels aren't deduped to people. But the order of magnitude is not in doubt.)

The email flows say the same thing in dollars. The post-cancel **reactivation flow** attributes about **\$32k**; the upstream **churn-prevention flow** attributes about **\$1.17M** across dozens of distinct messages. That's prevention out-earning post-cancel reactivation by roughly **37 to 1.**

**So the honest teaching is a hierarchy, not a hype.** Work the paused list — it's warm, it's cheap, the resume nudge is nearly free and the volume is climbing. But do not let a winback module fool you into thinking resurrection is where the growth is. The prevention flows keep twenty subscribers for every one this module wins back.

## The artifact — the paused-segment playbook

```text The playbook
SEGMENT PAUSED SEPARATELY FROM CANCELLED. Never merge the two into "churn."

STATE: pause scheduled to auto-resume
   → AUTO-RESUME NUDGE (no offer): "Next order [date] — change anything?"
     Goal: turn a silent resume into a welcomed one, pre-empt the charge-day cancel.

STATE: still has product / asked for room
   → PAUSE-EXTENSION or extra skip (no offer). Keep it alive at zero cost.
     Reactivation DISCOUNT here = margin donation. Don't.

STATE: pause gone cold, ignored the nudges
   → RESERVED reactivation offer (last lever, once).

MEASURE AGAINST THE RIGHT DENOMINATOR:
   reactivations + resumes recovered  ÷  cancellations in the same window.
   If that ratio is ~1:20 (this store), your leverage is UPSTREAM (prevention), not here.
```
