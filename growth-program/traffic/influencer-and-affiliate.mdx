**The anti-lesson this kills: "pay on first order like everyone else."**

Every affiliate template on the internet assumes the transaction is the end of the story: creator drives sale, creator gets X% of sale, done. But you're not buying sales — you're buying subscribers whose value arrives across a year of renewals. Pay creators like you're buying one-time sales and you get creators optimized for one-time buyers: discount-heavy, urgency-led, spike-shaped traffic that churns off the cohort table.

Structure it for recurring value and you recruit for recurring value.

## The decision — structure the deal for the revenue shape

(The split structures and tiering rule are operator calls; the data supplies the referral case and the tracking mechanism.)

**The split menu**, from safest-for-you to strongest-for-creators:

1. **First-order commission, higher rate** — familiar to creators, capped exposure. The tourist trap: it rewards volume regardless of quality. Use it only with the cohort audit (below).
2. **First-order + subscription bonus** — base rate on the sale, a fixed bonus when the customer's order is a subscription start. The bonus points the creator's creative at the subscribe button, which is where you wanted it anyway. This is the workhorse structure — simple enough for any creator, aimed at the right event.
3. **Lifetime split, lower rate** — a small percentage of the subscriber's recurring revenue for N months. The strongest alignment: the creator now profits from cohort QUALITY, not just volume — and it self-finances from revenue that only exists if the subscriber stays. Reserve for proven creators; the accounting is real work, and an unproven creator on a lifetime split is complexity purchased before trust.
4. **Hybrid tiers** — start creators on structure 2; graduate the ones whose cohorts hold to structure 3. Graduation is the incentive to send quality, and [your cohort table](/growth-program/numbers/cohorts) is the graduation exam.

**The measured case — referral, the quietest creator channel.** The data's strongest row in this whole territory isn't an influencer at all: at Brand A, the **customer referral program drove ~5,600 transactions at 5.7% session CVR (~\$720k attributed)** *(GA4 directional traffic, not ad economics; single store)* — conversion quality far above cold social's 0.7%, because the "creator" is a friend with the product on their counter. And at Brand B, the membership's built-in referral credit makes referral a standing perk rather than a campaign. The teaching: **build the referral program BEFORE chasing influencers** — it's the same mechanism (a trusted voice, a tracked code) with the highest-trust voice available and commission paid in credit that recycles into retention.

**Tracking that survives multi-touch.** The subscription journey is long — ad, podcast, search, email, then purchase — and last-click will lie to you about creators specifically. The data shows the durable answer working at scale: **code-level tracking.** Brand B's ledger carries a long tail of creator/podcast/event codes *(mechanism evidence — no per-creator economics exist in the data)*, and codes survive every touch: whoever's code gets typed gets credit, regardless of path. Give every creator a unique code AND a unique link, reconcile monthly, and accept the honest truth that some overlap is unknowable — pay the code.

**Which creators drive subscribers vs spikes:** answer it with the cohort audit, not the content aesthetic. Run each creator's code through the cohort table quarterly: 60/90/180-day repeat by creator code. Creators whose cohorts hold like your evergreen offer's (~50% at 180d at Brand A) get graduated and renewed; creators whose cohorts decay like a deep-discount spike get the structure-1 rate or the goodbye. You will find both kinds wearing identical follower counts.

## The proof (and its honest edge)

Measured: the referral program's conversion quality (GA4 directional, single store); the creator-code long tail as a working attribution mechanism; membership referral credit as a standing motion. NOT measured: any per-creator economics, any commission structure's performance, any influencer campaign's cohort quality. The split menu and tiering rule are operator frameworks; the cohort audit is how YOU generate the data this page can't hand you.

## The artifact — the creator deal-structure decision tree

```text The decision tree
NEW CREATOR, UNPROVEN
  → Structure 2 (first-order + subscription bonus), unique code + link
  → 90 days, then the cohort audit

THE COHORT AUDIT (quarterly, per creator code)
  Cohort repeat at 90/180d ≥ your evergreen-offer baseline?
    YES → graduate: offer structure 3 (lifetime split, N months) or raise the bonus
    NO, and volume is real → structure 1 rate; they're a spike channel, price them as one
    NO, and volume is thin → end it kindly; the code stays live so you never lose the read

BEFORE ANY OF THIS
  [ ] The customer referral program exists and pays (cash or credit)
  [ ] Referral is offered post-purchase and in the portal (the credit loop)
  [ ] Every creator has a unique code (survives multi-touch) + unique link
  [ ] Someone owns the monthly reconcile; the code wins disputes

THE LINE YOU HOLD
  [ ] No lifetime split before a passed cohort audit — alignment is earned
  [ ] No paying for follower counts; you pay for cohorts
```
