**The anti-lesson this kills: "more dashboards = more control."** ([Weekly metrics](/growth-program/numbers/weekly-metrics) already killed it once — this lesson makes the survivor operational.)

By this point in the school you own more instruments than most platforms ship: a unit-economics one-pager, a churn decomposition, a cohort table, a weekly dashboard spec, a testing doctrine, a lifecycle map, a holdout design. The failure mode now isn't ignorance — it's the instrument museum: everything built, nothing scheduled, decisions still made by vibe in the Slack channel where the loudest anomaly wins. A rhythm is what turns instruments into an operating system.

A dashboard nobody's scheduled to read is a screensaver.

## The decision — three cadences, three altitudes

(The split and the one-decision rule are operating doctrine; the data supplies what each altitude actually catches.)

**WEEKLY — the dashboard, the anomalies, ONE decision.** The [weekly dashboard](/growth-program/numbers/weekly-metrics) (starts, cancels, save/resume ratio, recurring share, failed payments), read in fifteen minutes, hunting one thing: what moved that shouldn't have. What a weekly read actually catches, illustrated from the data: recurring share at Brand A swung between ~41% and ~60% of paid orders by season — BFCM diluted it with new-customer surges *(tag-proxy hedge; single store)*. An operator without the rhythm sees November's recurring share drop and panics at phantom churn; the operator with the rhythm knows dilution-by-success from erosion, because they've watched the number weekly through a full season. The weekly decision is small by design: investigate X, or don't. Nothing strategic moves weekly — that's the point; the weekly protects the monthly from noise.

**MONTHLY — cohorts, flows, offer health.** The three reviews that matter, each owing its decision:

- **Cohort review:** which acquisition cohorts hit 60-day on-pace, which matured to 180-day verdict. Decision: does any offer's cohort trend demand a change to [the testing queue](/growth-program/cadence/testing-calendar)?
- **Flow performance** ([the lifecycle map](/growth-program/messaging/lifecycle-map) as checklist): attributed value and unsub pressure by flow family — is the lifecycle engine compounding or plateauing? Decision: which ONE flow gets rebuilt or extended this month?
- **Offer health:** starts by offer, depth cost, any drift toward oscillation. Decision: does the current test read clean, or does the calendar hold?

**QUARTERLY — the strategy pass: pricing, perks, and what to kill.** The quarterly is where you're allowed to touch the big dials — [the ongoing rate](/growth-program/offer/the-discount-question), [perk economics](/growth-program/account-area/perks-and-loyalty), [membership structure](/growth-program/offer/memberships) — precisely because you didn't touch them for thirteen weeks. And the discipline nobody schedules: **the kill list.** Every quarter, one thing dies on purpose — the perk nobody redeems, the flow that stopped earning its unsub pressure, the creator code that failed two cohort audits. Growth work accretes; the quarterly is the only cadence with the altitude to prune.

## The proof (and its honest edge)

The data can't show you a team's operating rhythm — no such data exists anywhere. What it shows is why each altitude exists: seasonal swings that only a weekly baseline makes readable (the 41–60% recurring band), maturity windows that force monthly patience (60d on-pace, 180d verdict), and slow drifts only a quarter reveals (the year-over-year flow-value compounding vs the broadcast plateau). The cadences aren't preference; they're matched to the speeds at which subscription truths surface.

## The artifact — the operating calendar

| Cadence | Review | Inputs (built already) | The one decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Weekly** (15 min) | The dashboard read — starts, cancels, save/resume, recurring share, failed payments | The weekly dashboard | Investigate the anomaly, or don't |
| **Monthly** (90 min) | Cohort review · Flow performance · Offer health | Cohort table · lifecycle map + flow report · offer worksheet + test status | Change the testing queue, or hold · the ONE flow to rebuild · test reads clean, or calendar holds |
| **Quarterly** (half day) | The strategy pass — pricing, perks, membership, kill list | Rate math, perk audit, membership viability, cohort trends, holdout results | Move a big dial, or explicitly don't — **plus THE KILL: one thing dies** |

```text The rules
[ ] Every review ends with its decision written down (one line, dated)
[ ] No big dial moves outside the quarterly (emergencies excepted, then documented)
[ ] The weekly protects the monthly from noise; the monthly protects the
    quarterly from drift; the quarterly protects the year from accretion
```
