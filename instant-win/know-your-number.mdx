## The math

Then two more lines:

- × 12 = what this costs you a year.
- × 60 = what it costs you over five years — the number that belongs in the same mental drawer as your equipment leases and your 3PL contract.

Don't use the advertised rate. Use the invoice. The advertised rate is the floor; your blended rate — with the per-order fees, the messaging meters, the add-ons — is the truth.

## Now read your contract

Five minutes that change the negotiation. Find four things in your Order Form:

1. Your term — month-to-month, or 12 months?
2. Your renewal date — and does it auto-renew? (It almost certainly does.)
3. The non-renewal notice window — 30 days is the industry norm. Miss it and you're re-committed for a full term.
4. Any lock-in or early-termination language. Recharge Plus, for instance: leave mid-term and the published formula is every remaining month's fees, due now. Loop: the lock-in RESETS every time you renew.

Can't find an Order Form? Then you're likely month-to-month — which means you can leave next month, and that is the strongest negotiating position in this entire kit. If you're on a vendor that publishes no terms at all (Stay AI publishes none), everything is negotiable, in writing, right now.

Write the final line down:

```text Your line
My platform takes $______ a year. My renewal is ______.
My notice window closes ______.
```
