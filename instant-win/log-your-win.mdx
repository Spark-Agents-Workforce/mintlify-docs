One row. Keep it somewhere you'll find it in eleven months:

```text The row
Date sent · Script used · Reply (1–7) · Old blended rate · New rate ·
Annualized saving · Next renewal window opens
```

Three reasons this row matters more than it looks:

## 1 · It's your receipt

At renewal time — theirs and ours — this row is the whole conversation. You'll know exactly what the kit returned against what the membership cost, in one line of arithmetic.

## 2 · It's the wins wall

  <img src="/images/scenes/wins-wall.jpg" alt="The wins wall taking shape — dollar receipts pinned row by row, the newest one starred" style={{ width: "100%", margin: 0 }} noZoom />

Post the row — the savings, not your confidential rate terms. (Some vendor contracts, Loop's among them, literally bar you from sharing negotiated pricing; the SAVING is yours to shout.) Every posted row makes the next member's Script 1 land harder — and builds the first real, documented record of these fees actually moving. The industry has never had one. We're building it on a wall.

## 3 · It's next year's opener

Script 2, next cycle, opens with this year's row. "Last year we agreed to X; the market has moved again" is the strongest opening position a returning negotiator has.
