```text Subject: Subscription platform costs — what I found
[NAME] —

I ran our subscription platform bill through a worksheet. We pay an
effective [BLENDED]% of subscription revenue — about [$X] a year,
[$5X] over five years. That fee scales with our revenue; the work
behind it doesn't (the platform schedules billing; Shopify processes
the payments — we pay Shopify separately for that).

Options, in order of effort:
1. Negotiated rate match: worst case nothing, documented average for
   disciplined SaaS negotiations is ~16.5% (Vendr, 20k+ deals).
   In motion.
2. Move to the lowest published competitor: saves [$Y]/yr, migration
   risk is card-preserving by Shopify's architecture.
3. Self-host (we hold a license): fee goes to 0%, runs ~$45/mo in
   infrastructure. Kills the line item permanently — [$X]/yr straight
   back to EBITDA, and it stops scaling against us.

Recommending we run 1 now and evaluate 3 this quarter.

[NAME]
```

**Note what this memo does quietly:** it converts the platform fee from "a cost of doing subscriptions" into "an EBITDA line item with three exits," which is the sentence CFOs act on. No CAPEX framing; the fee kill is presented as what it is — permanent OpEx elimination that stops scaling with growth.
