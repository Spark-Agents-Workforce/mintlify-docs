One honest thing first: merchants who ask for fee relief without leverage get form letters. The public record is full of "hard no and a boilerplate." That's not a reason to skip this — it's the proof that the leverage is the whole game. You're not asking nicely. You're presenting alternatives you hold.

Across the industry, disciplined SaaS negotiation with real alternatives on the table averages 16.5% savings ([Vendr's figure](https://www.vendr.com), across 20,000+ deals). Your result depends on your leverage and your timing. Here's every reply you'll get, decoded:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='1 · "Our pricing reflects the value of the platform."'>
    **Translation:** the opening deflection; costs them nothing.

    **Your move:** don't argue value — re-anchor. "Understood — and Loop publishes 0.75% with $0 per order for the same category of value. Can you review the rate or not?" One sentence. Hold.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title='2 · "We could look at something with an annual commitment."'>
    **Translation:** real movement — they're trading term for rate.

    **Your move:** the counter-trade rule. A longer term is worth giving ONLY if the rate hits your target — and on month-to-month plans, remember a term also locks the rate against mid-term hikes, which has real value. "If [TARGET]% is on the table, we can discuss a 12-month term." Never accept term-for-small-discount.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title='3 · "That rate is enterprise-only / for larger accounts."'>
    **Translation:** a tier defense.

    **Your move:** "The 0.75% I referenced is Loop's published Pro plan — $399 a month, public page, not an enterprise quote. What can you do at our volume?" (This is why the anchor is a published rate: this reply dies against a URL.)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title='4 · "That&apos;s not an apples-to-apples comparison."'>
    **Translation:** the spec dodge — the standard counter to any benchmark.

    **Your move:** make them name it. "Which capability in our current usage does the comparison miss? Happy to check whether we use it." Nine times in ten the difference they name is something you don't use — which strengthens your case.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title='5 · "Let me loop in your account manager / CSM."'>
    **Translation:** the handoff — sometimes progress, often a parking lot.

    **Your move:** accept it WITH a date. "Great — given our renewal timing, can we have their answer by [DATE]?" If the date slips once, [Script 3](/instant-win/the-scripts#script-3-·-the-escalation).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="6 · Silence.">
    **Translation:** the most common reply of all.

    **Your move:** day 4, one line on the same thread ("Bumping this — renewal math is happening on our side this month."). Day 10: [Script 3](/instant-win/the-scripts#script-3-·-the-escalation). Renewal window closing: send your non-renewal notice ON TIME regardless — notice is revocable in practice, renewal terms aren't.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title='7 · "Best we can do is [X]."'>
    **Translation:** the real number. Negotiation over; arithmetic begins.

    **Your move:** if X ≥ your target — take it, [log it](/instant-win/log-your-win), calendar next year. If X < target: "Appreciate you running it down. We'll take the rest of the value to zero ourselves." And mean it — [If they say no](/instant-win/if-they-say-no).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
