Three emails. They are short on purpose — field data on millions of emails says a 50-to-100-word ask outperforms a thorough one, by a lot. Every extra paragraph you add is a place for your ask to drown. Resist.

The send order: Script 1 opens. Script 2 is Script 1 timed to your renewal window (use it instead if your renewal is 60–90 days out — check the line you wrote in [Know your number](/instant-win/know-your-number)). Script 3 escalates if the answer is no or nothing.

One rule before you paste: everything in these emails is true and checkable. That's not a compliance note — it's the mechanism. A vendor can smell a bluff; these contain none.

## Script 1 · The rate review

```text Subject: Rate review request — [STORE NAME]
Hi [NAME],

Loop publishes 0.75% with no per-order fee on their Pro plan. We're
currently paying an effective [YOUR BLENDED RATE]% with [VENDOR] —
I've done the math off our last invoice.

We're set up to self-host our subscription infrastructure at 0%, so
we're reviewing our options. Before we go further down that path:
can you match [TARGET RATE]% with the per-order fee waived?

Happy to share the invoice math. Who's the right person if that's
above your desk?

[NAME]
```

**Why it works, in one breath:** the published competitor rate lands first (the anchor), your blended rate makes it personal, the self-host line is a capability you actually hold — not a threat, an inventory item — and the ask is one number with an escalation path built in.

**Do not add:** your budget, your timeline, how much you like the product, or a second ask. Any of those hands them the email back.

**[TARGET RATE]:** aim at or just below the published competitor anchor. A number with a reason beats a round number without one — "0.7%, given Loop publishes 0.75% and charges nothing per order" is a rate with a rationale.

## Script 2 · The renewal window

    <span style={{ backgroundColor: "#000", color: "#FFDE00", fontWeight: 800, fontSize: "11px", textTransform: "uppercase", padding: "2px 8px" }}>Send 60–90 days before renewal</span>
  </div>

```text Subject: Before our renewal on [DATE] — [STORE NAME]
Hi [NAME],

Our term renews on [DATE]. Before it does, we're deciding between
renewing, moving to a published-lower-rate platform (Loop Pro:
0.75%, $0 per order), and self-hosting at 0% — we hold the
infrastructure for that option.

We'd rather stay, at the right rate. Can you get us to
[TARGET RATE]% with the per-order fee waived before
[DATE MINUS NOTICE WINDOW]? That's our notice deadline, so we'll
need an answer before it.

[NAME]
```

**The date does the work.** You're not threatening; you're scheduling. Their notice window is now YOUR deadline, pointed back at them.

**Send 60–90 days out.** Procurement data is unambiguous: negotiations opened early save multiples of those opened at the deadline. If you're reading this mid-term, calendar it now — that's today's play.

    <span style={{ backgroundColor: "#000", color: "#FFDE00", fontWeight: 800, fontSize: "11px", textTransform: "uppercase", padding: "2px 8px" }}>Send after a no — or 10 days of silence</span>

```text Subject: Commercial review — [STORE NAME] ([$X]/yr account)
Hi [NAME],

[REP NAME] and I discussed our rate on [DATE]; we haven't found a
path. Before we finalize a migration decision, I wanted to put this
in front of someone with pricing authority.

The numbers: we pay [VENDOR] about [$X] a year. Loop publishes
0.75% with no per-order fee. Our self-hosted option runs at 0%.
Replacing our account costs you more than the rate match we're
asking for: [TARGET RATE]%.

If that's workable, we'll stay and this is done this week. If not,
no hard feelings — we'll plan the transition.

[NAME]
```

**This is a commercial position, not a complaint.** It never criticizes the rep (you may need that relationship on the way out). It states the replacement-cost logic in one line and gives them a clean, fast yes.

**Route it up:** reply-all with the rep's manager added, or the "who's the right person" answer from Script 1. Reps typically hold small discounts; real rate moves live at the deal desk.

## The timing box

When to move, by platform — from their own published terms:

- **Month-to-month (Recharge Starter, most self-serve tiers):** anytime. You can leave next month and they know it. Send Script 1 today.
- **Recharge Plus/Custom (12-month terms):** your window is BEFORE renewal — mid-term exit costs every remaining month, by formula, per their ToS. Open at T-minus-90. Downgrades also only land at expiry.
- **Skio / Loop / Smartrr (auto-renew + 30-day notice):** calendar T-minus-90 for the open, T-minus-35 as your hard deadline — miss the notice window and you're re-upped. Loop members: your lock-in RESETS at renewal, and Loop's terms bar running a competitor app during your term — so any migration test gets scheduled for after non-renewal, which makes the calendar even more load-bearing.
- **Stay AI:** no published terms — your leverage is whatever your Order Form says, so read it, and put everything you care about in writing at renewal.
- **Everyone:** mid-contract with no window in sight? Send Script 1 anyway. Rate reviews aren't contract-gated. The worst case is a no you were already living with.
