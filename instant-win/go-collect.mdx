## The questions, answered

**Why do you pay a percentage of revenue?**

→ Because in 2014 the fee bought a checkout and a payments stack. Shopify took [that work over in 2020](/instant-win/the-history). The fee stayed. That's it. That's the whole story.

**What does the 19 cents pay for?**

→ [A transaction your platform doesn't process](/instant-win/what-your-platform-does).

**What does the machine actually cost to run?**

→ [About $45 a month](/instant-win/what-your-platform-does#what-running-one-actually-costs). You saw the line items.

## What you're holding now

- **YOUR NUMBER** — [what they actually take per year](/instant-win/know-your-number), off your real invoice.
- **YOUR DATE** — [the renewal window where your leverage peaks](/instant-win/the-scripts#the-timing-box), calendared.
- **YOUR ALTERNATIVE** — [deployable infrastructure you own](/instant-win/the-reveal). "I can take this to zero" is a fact, not a bluff.
- **YOUR WORDS** — [three emails](/instant-win/the-scripts), [seven counters](/instant-win/what-theyll-say-back), [one CFO memo](/instant-win/the-cfo-memo). Paste-ready.

## The play

Compute the number. Calendar the date. Send the email. Handle the reply. Log the win. Repeat every year — next year it costs you one afternoon.

And every branch wins. Rate cut? Money. Stall? Escalate. Hard no? They just wrote your migration business case, in writing, themselves.

Most merchants never ask — that's why form letters work on them. You're not most merchants anymore. You know what the machine does, what it costs, and that you can leave. Vendors can smell the difference.
