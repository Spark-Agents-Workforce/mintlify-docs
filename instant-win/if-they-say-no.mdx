A firm no is not a failure. It's information — and it's the most expensive sentence your vendor will ever send you.

Here's what a no means: they've priced their confidence that you can't leave. Every month you stay after a no, you're paying the trapped-customer rate, and now you both know it.

Two exits, both good:

## Exit A — Bank and calendar

Take whatever they did move (a waived fee, a partial match — it all counts), [log it](/instant-win/log-your-win), and put the next renewal window on your calendar with a 90-day lead. The kit is annual. So are we. Rate reviews compound.

## Exit B — The door they built

You've read [how the machine works](/instant-win/what-your-platform-does). You know it costs utility-bill money to run. The platform, the migration tool, and the walkthroughs are all one tab over — and the no you're holding is the business case, written by the vendor. Either they cut your rate, or they explained, in writing, exactly why you should stop paying it.
